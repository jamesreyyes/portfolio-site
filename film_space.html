Film Space: Unique Venue Booking Made Easy

Location-Based Recommendation App - UX/UI Case Study

Project Context

Film Space is a responsive web app that simplifies the location scouting process for film makers and other creatives, by delivering a platform that makes unique venue booking more easy and accessible.

Problem

Booking unique venues for photoshoots or film shoots can seem intimidating or stressful. For example, people in the industry gate keep locations to prevent the masses from using the same location. This can discourage young film makers or photographers from expanding their talents to a more professional atmosphere.

Solution

Film Space offers a friendly platform that allows users to easily search for locations based on their individual needs. (i.e., staying within budget or finding the correct atmosphere

My Design Process

I followed a Lean UX design process to help me gather information to create the final UI. I was fortunate to interview actual users to gather their input which enhanced the design process.

Product Research

In this stage of my design process, my goal was to investigate my users and their pain points, so I conducted a competitive analysis and User Interviews.

Analysis

I created user personas to illustrate an archetypical model of a user who will interact with my product. I then defined an MVP to work more efficiently and deliver to the pain points of my users.

Prototype

I created wireframes and conducted usability tests on my prototypes to help me in improving my early designs.

UI Design

I designed mid-fidelity wireframes based on the analysis from the usability tests. Then I created a mood board and style guide to add the graphical interface to the app.

Competitive Analysis

I began this competitive analysis by defining each competiive overview, which highlights the competitor's core message, overall strategy, and market advantage. I then ran a SWOT analysis on each competitor to analyze each of these aspects for my competitor profile.

Roadtripper

Competitive Overview

Roadtripper identifies themselves as "the #1 road trip planner" because of the simplicity of the app and the data base it holds. Roadtripper aims to give its users an "authentic experience" when it comes to road trips. Their mission is to help users escape the toursist bubble by leading you to the road less traveled.

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • Strong data base of points of interests.
  • Large user base that contributes to the content of the app.

Weaknesses

  • Expensive for the pro version.
  • Reports of the app being "glitchy".

Opportunities

  • Focus on backend development to improve the function of the app.
  • Run ads on social media sites such as YouTube, Facebook, and Instagram to improve brand visibility.

Threats

  • App works similarly to Google Maps which offers navigation and point of interest pit stops, while being free.
  • Free travel blogs that highlight hidden gems in the area.

PeerSpace

Competitive Overview

Peerspace is a peer-to-peer marketplace for booking space for events, meetings, and productions. On their landing page they pride themselves with the ability to "book professional shoots with ease."

SWOT Analysis

Strengths

  • Easy to find on Google and maintains a high rating on the app store.
  • The functions of the app are easy to use.

Weaknesses

  • UI feels stale
  • Not many outside articles on Peerspace, marketing could be improved.

Opportunities

  • Update the design.
  • Run a marketing campaign with film maker influencers to increase brand awareness to a similar audience.

Threats

  • Similar apps, such as Giggster.
  • Other venue booking apps that offer more locations with additional accommodations, such as renting a vehicle for a film shoot.

User Interviews

To further understand my audience, I prepared user interviews and recruited participants that would gain me high quality insight. In my case, I recruited people who enjoy film making and photography. After conducting the interviews, I synthesized their data to determine any patterns and key takeaways.

Questions

  1. Have you rented out a production space before or been to one? (If so, what was the process like?)
  2. When it comes to film making or photography, how important is the location?
  3. Where would you normally go to shoot?
  4. Have you had difficulty in searching for help for a project?
  5. Have you got into trouble when shooting in a public space?

Synthesis

  • All interviewees answered that they ran into trouble when filming in a public area.
  • Main frustrations were not having the budget to rent out a production space.
  • Interviewees mentioned they would like budget friendly options, or show free public spaces that do not require a film permit.

User Personas

After analyzing my user interviews, I was able to get a better understanding of my potential audience. This allowed me to create user personas. These personas helped me understand that different people have different needs and expectations.

Minimum Viable Product (MVP)

To work more efficiently and maximize my resources, I went for a Lean UX approach and defined a MVP. My MVP document includes jobs to be done and their associated features.

MVP: "Create an app that simplifies the process of unique venue booking"

Usability Test

To measure the efficiency of the overall design for Film Space, I conducted a usability test with actual users. I gave the participants a set of tasks to complete and had them verbally explain their thought process. After conducting the tests with the participants, I synthesized their comments to aid me in improving my prototypes.

Tasks

  1. Get to browse film spaces page.
  2. Filter selection by theme.
  3. View Reviews.
  4. View liked page.

Synthesis

Issue Error Rating Suggestions
Too many pages to reach browsing screen 3 While conducting tests, it was apparent that users thought too much information was needed to arrive at the browsing page. I could streamline the process and include everything on one page instead of two pages.
Too many filtering options 2 While on the browsing screen, one user noted that the filtering options could be cleaned up. I agree that there's too many options for filtering and it could be cleaned up to look less cluttered.

Retrospective

What Went Well

I’m quite happy with my overall design and I’m proud of this project being my first full-length UX/UI design project. Going into this case, I wanted to design a tool that solves a problem I am familiar with. So I chose to design Film Space because I am familiar with the pain points of the film making audience. I was able to communicate my problem statement well and with the help of my user testers, I was able to come up with a proposed design solution.

What didn't go well

Initially I went into this project with a mobile-first approach, and I felt it was difficult to make my designs responsive to web and tablet screens. I think practicing designing different responsive apps would help me overcome this, as well as doing more research on current popular responsive web apps, such as Airbnb.

Future Considerations

Moving forward I want to continue getting constant feedback throughout my design process like how I managed to do with this case. Even though this was a self-made UX/UI case, I enjoyed receiving constant feedback through user interviews and user testing to keep my motivation high and help me through any parts where I felt stuck.
